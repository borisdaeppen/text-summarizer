What else should I know about Organic tracking?

Just a few little things, especially when testing …
 
1.    Unlike with tracking links and rotators where everything is reported in real-time, there’s up to a 1 minute delay before an Organic click will be reflected in your stats.
 
2.    If a user has already reached your site via a tracking link, any “organic” clicks from that user are ignored and conversions are attributed to the tracking link as usual.

This goes for you as well, so if you’re trying to generate “test” organic clicks you’ll want to use another browser, use an “incognito” browser window, or see the FAQ below about clearing your ClickMagick cookies:
 
  How can I easily delete my cookies for testing?
   
3.    Organic traffic coming from secure websites will show up under the Referrer Name of “[None]” because secure websites are not allowed to pass referrer information.

If you are expecting traffic from a certain domain and you’re not seeing it, look to see if the domain is a secure domain. A common example of this is YouTube.

YouTube is a secure domain (look for the https in the URL), therefore all organic traffic from YouTube will be reported anonymously under “[None]”.
