Why is a ClickMagick domain blacklisted?

ClickMagick’s generic tracking domains are whitelisted with most “big” sites like Google and Facebook.

But occasionally clkmg.com and/or clkmr.com will temporarily end up on one of the various anti-spam “blacklists” because one of our users sent “spam” …

… or because the blacklist maintainer made a mistake (known as a “false positive”), which is also common.

We monitor all the major blacklists, and any time a domain is blacklisted we’re usually able to take care of it within hours, so this should never really be a problem.

If one of our generic domains IS indeed blacklisted and it’s preventing you from doing something or causing you a problem, just wait a few hours and the problem will usually be resolved. If not, contact us for an update.

The easiest way to avoid being affected in the rare time when one of our domains is temporarily blacklisted is to use your own custom tracking domain. A Note About URIBL

URIBL is a realtime URI blacklist, which means it’s fully automated. And it has TONS of “false positives” because they don’t actually verify if an email is truly spam or not.

This is the only blacklist that our domains are regularly listed on, and there’s just nothing we can do about it because their “service” is flat-out faulty.

Unfortunately some websites and services integrate it into their systems without really knowing how URIBL works – and how inaccurate it is.

Simply wait 2-12 hours and the problem will go away when the URIBL listing expires automatically.

If you use a service that monitors URIBL listings and this is frequently a problem for you, the solution is to simply set up a custom tracking domain.
